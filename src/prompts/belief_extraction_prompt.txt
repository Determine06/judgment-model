You extract structured judgment signals from raw question-answer pairs.

Your task is to read:
1. a question
2. a raw spoken-style answer

and return:
- one verdict
- 2 to 4 canonical beliefs that support that verdict

You are NOT summarizing the answer generally.
You are extracting the underlying judgment.

-----------------------------------
DEFINITIONS
-----------------------------------

Verdict:
The single main conclusion of the answer.
It should be concise, declarative, and capture the actual final judgment.

Canonical belief:
A reusable principle, causal judgment, evaluative rule, or development principle that helps explain why the verdict is true.

A canonical belief should be:
- general enough to recur across multiple answers
- faithful to the answer
- written as a clean declarative sentence
- focused on one idea only

-----------------------------------
WHAT TO EXTRACT
-----------------------------------

Extract exactly:
- 1 verdict
- 2 to 4 canonical beliefs
Prefer 3 beliefs when possible.

-----------------------------------
VERDICT RULES
-----------------------------------

The verdict must:
- be one short sentence
- directly reflect the answer's conclusion
- not be a copy of the question
- not contain unnecessary question details
- not be vague or generic
- not include filler like "I think" or "probably" unless the answer is genuinely uncertain

Good verdict style:
- "The real issue is weak game-speed ball control."
- "Shooting should be the first priority."
- "Weak-hand control matters more early on."
- "The missing factor is the mental side of the game."

Bad verdict style:
- "The answer is that the player should focus on the thing discussed in the question."
- "Basketball improvement depends on many things."
- "He thinks that maybe confidence could matter."

-----------------------------------
CANONICAL BELIEF RULES
-----------------------------------

Each canonical belief must:
- express one main idea only
- support the verdict
- be more reusable than the raw wording
- retain the key constraint, mechanism, or tradeoff from the answer
- preserve the speaker's meaning
- avoid rhetorical filler
- avoid direct restatement of the verdict
- avoid examples unless the example itself is the principle
- avoid copying the question wording

Canonical beliefs should usually look like:
- "Game skills must transfer under real speed and pressure."
- "Tight control matters more than flashy ball handling."
- "Confidence affects whether practiced skills appear in games."
- "Fundamentals create more long-term value than advanced moves early."
- "Explosiveness and footwork matter more for separation than dribble volume."
- "Shooting is the easiest scalable scoring skill for smaller players."

-----------------------------------
BELIEF QUALITY REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------

Canonical beliefs should capture decision-making logic, not just general truths.

Prefer beliefs that:
- express a tradeoff (X matters more than Y)
- express a condition (X fails when Y happens)
- express a mechanism (X leads to Y because Z)
- express a constraint (X only works if Y)

Avoid beliefs that are obvious or surface-level outcomes.

Bad belief:
- "Effective shooting leads to more points."

Better belief:
- "Shooting creates scalable scoring because it does not depend on dribble creation."

Bad belief:
- "Ball control is important."

Better belief:
- "Ball control must hold up under defensive pressure to be usable in games."

-----------------------------------
DO NOT EXTRACT
-----------------------------------

Do NOT output:
- anecdotes
- named-player examples unless absolutely required
- motivational filler
- surface evidence
- repeated paraphrases of the same idea
- direct copies of the raw answer
- beliefs that are not actually present in the answer
- generic statements that could apply to anything

Do NOT turn these into beliefs:
- "Luka does not dribble fast."
- "Kyrie Irving uses flashy moves."
- "Read this mental toughness book."

Instead extract the underlying principle:
- "Tight control matters more than dribble speed."
- "A move is valuable only if it creates game impact."
- "Mental strength affects game performance."

-----------------------------------
IMPORTANT DISTINCTIONS
-----------------------------------

A belief is a reusable principle, not just a detail.

If the answer gives a drill, try to extract the principle behind the drill unless the drill itself is clearly the core answer.

Example:
Raw answer: "Use Mikan drills."
Better belief:
- "Weak-hand and opposite-side finishing improve through deliberate two-hand repetition."

Raw answer: "Use two balls or a tennis ball."
Better belief:
- "Ball handling improves when dribbling becomes automatic under divided attention."

-----------------------------------
DEPTH VS GENERICITY
-----------------------------------

If a belief becomes too generic, rewrite it to include:
- a condition
- a tradeoff
- or a mechanism

Always prefer a slightly more specific belief over a generic one.

Example:
Generic:
- "Confidence affects performance."

Better:
- "Lack of confidence prevents players from applying practiced skills in games."

-----------------------------------
ANTI-GENERIC RULE
-----------------------------------

Before outputting beliefs, check:

Could this belief apply to almost any situation in basketball?

If yes, rewrite it to be more specific to the reasoning in the answer.

-----------------------------------
STYLE RULES
-----------------------------------

All output text must be:
- concise
- clean
- declarative
- faithful to the answer
- free of markdown
- free of commentary

Do not explain your choices.
Do not include any text outside the JSON.

-----------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT
-----------------------------------

Return strict JSON with exactly these keys:

{
  "verdict": "string",
  "canonical_beliefs": [
    "string",
    "string"
  ]
}

Rules:
- canonical_beliefs must contain 2 to 4 strings
- prefer 3 when possible
- no duplicate beliefs
- no empty strings
- no extra keys
- no markdown code fences

INPUT
-----------------------------------

Question:
{{question}}

Raw Answer:
{{raw_answer}}
